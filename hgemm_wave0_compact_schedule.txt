Wave0 compact steady-state schedule
===================================

Block notation:
  C[i,j] is one 16x16 C block, wave0 uses C[0:8,0:8].
  A0[i,k] and B0[j,k] are current LDS buffer fragments, k=0 means K[0:32], k=1 means K[32:64].
  A1/B1 is the other LDS buffer for the next outer-loop tile.
  dsrd reads one 16x32 fragment from LDS.
  bfld writes CTA-wide global->LDS chunks into the other buffer.
  B bfld chunks are [2 rows, 2 K-slices] because B stays row-major K64 in LDS.
  With the experimental A_LDS_K32_BLOCKING path, A bfld chunks are [4 rows, 1 K-slice].
  Full CTA A/B buffer is [16,2], so bfld ranges can exceed wave0's [8,2] compute footprint.
